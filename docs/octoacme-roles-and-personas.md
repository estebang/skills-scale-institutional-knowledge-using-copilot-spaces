# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers focus on creating intuitive, user-centered experiences. They design workflows, create prototypes, and validate usability through user research and testing. They ensure that features meet user needs and business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Develop wireframes, mockups, and user flows for new features
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Validate that deliverables meet usability and accessibility standards
- Partner with Product Managers on user-centered solutions
- Collaborate with Developers to ensure design feasibility and implementation fidelity
- Create and maintain design systems and style guides

### Goals
- Drive user adoption and satisfaction through effective design
- Reduce rework caused by usability gaps discovered late
- Ensure consistent user experience across all features
- Validate design decisions with user feedback and data

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- Usability testing sessions and feedback reports
- Stakeholder presentations of design concepts
- Collaboration in sprint planning to scope design work

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on feature requirements and user stories; validate that designs align with product vision
- **Developers**: Work closely during implementation to ensure design feasibility and provide clarifications
- **QA/Testing**: Partner on usability testing and acceptance criteria validation
- **Stakeholders**: Present design concepts and gather feedback during reviews

---

## QA Automation Engineer

### Role Summary
QA Automation Engineers build and maintain automated test frameworks to ensure consistent test coverage and software reliability. They work to shift testing left in the development process and enable faster, more confident releases.

### Responsibilities
- Design, build, and maintain automated test suites (unit, integration, end-to-end)
- Collaborate with Developers to improve code testability and test coverage
- Integrate automated tests into CI/CD pipelines
- Track and report defects, test results, and quality metrics
- Identify test gaps and recommend improvements to testing strategy
- Maintain test data and test environments

### Goals
- Increase confidence in releases through comprehensive test coverage
- Enable early detection of defects and regressions
- Reduce manual testing effort through effective automation
- Improve overall software quality and reliability

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning to estimate testing effort
- Daily standups to report on test status and blockers
- Test reports and quality dashboards in CI/CD
- Collaboration with Developers on test strategy

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test strategy, code testability, and debugging failures
- **Project Managers**: Provide quality metrics and testing status updates
- **Release Manager**: Coordinate on test execution for release candidates
- **Product Managers**: Clarify acceptance criteria and edge cases for testing

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers oversee the end-to-end release process, ensuring that software is deployed safely, predictably, and with clear communication. They coordinate across teams to manage release cycles, documentation, and incident response.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule release cycles and deployment windows
- Coordinate release activities across development, QA, and operations teams
- Document release notes, migration guides, and known issues
- Manage release branches, tags, and versioning
- Ensure rollback plans and runbooks are prepared
- Coordinate communication about releases to stakeholders and support teams
- Monitor post-deployment and coordinate incident response if needed

### Goals
- Enable predictable, low-risk releases
- Minimize production incidents related to deployments
- Ensure fast incident response and recovery for release issues
- Maintain clear communication and documentation for all releases

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with PM, QA, and DevOps
- Release announcements to stakeholders and support teams
- Post-deployment status updates
- Incident coordination during release issues

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate release timelines with project milestones
- **QA Automation Engineers**: Ensure all required tests pass before release
- **Developers**: Manage code freeze, branch merges, and deployment verification
- **Stakeholders**: Communicate release schedules, changes, and impacts
- **DevOps/Operations**: Coordinate deployment execution and monitoring

---

## Stakeholder Advocate

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Advocates serve as the liaison between project teams and business/user stakeholders. They ensure that stakeholder feedback is gathered, represented in planning, and that project decisions remain aligned with business goals.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and maintain relationships with key stakeholders
- Solicit and compile stakeholder input and feedback
- Maintain open communication channels for stakeholder concerns
- Represent stakeholder interests in planning and prioritization discussions
- Track stakeholder satisfaction and address concerns proactively
- Organize stakeholder review sessions and demos

### Goals
- Maximize stakeholder satisfaction and engagement
- Ensure business and user needs are addressed throughout the project lifecycle
- Reduce misalignment between project deliverables and stakeholder expectations
- Build trust and transparency with stakeholder communities

### Typical Communication
- Regular stakeholder feedback sessions and surveys
- Monthly stakeholder update meetings
- Ad-hoc communication for urgent stakeholder concerns
- Retrospective input on stakeholder engagement effectiveness

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Share stakeholder feedback to inform roadmap and priorities
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate stakeholder communication plans and updates
- **UX Designers**: Facilitate stakeholder participation in design reviews
- **Developers**: Communicate technical constraints and trade-offs to stakeholders
- **Release Manager**: Coordinate stakeholder communication for releases
